2026 REQUIREMENTS
16 points.
Every year.

Every licensed legal consultant on the Dubai roll must earn 16 CLPD points before 31 December each year. No extensions. No exceptions.

8
Mandatory activities
Face-to-face and e-learning sessions on prescribed legal topics. Minimum 2 sessions must be face-to-face. Ethics is compulsory for all.
8
Accredited activities
Any approved course from the CLPD catalogue. No single accredited activity may exceed 4 points. Up to 50% may be completed online.

What's changed this year

New podcast series — Q2
On-demand audio content on emerging legal topics. Counts toward your accredited activities. Stay informed on the go.
DIFC Days introduced
In-person sessions in the DIFC. Complete all mandatory requirements in a single streamlined day at the Gate Village.
50% online now allowed
Up to 4 of your 8 mandatory points (50%) can now be completed online, for greater flexibility. Ethics remains compulsory.

ACCREDITED CLPD ACTIVITY

Voluntary Legal Services
Pro Bono

Earn accredited CLPD points by volunteering your legal expertise through the LAD Voluntary Legal Services Smart Portal. Connect with members of the public seeking legal assistance.

Access the Pro Bono Portal →
How it works
The Voluntary Legal Services Smart Portal connects members of the public seeking legal assistance with volunteering advocates and legal consultants across the Emirate of Dubai. Register and take appointments directly through the portal.
Points earned
4
COMPLETED APPOINTMENTS = 1 POINT
Four completed appointments through the portal = 1 Accredited CLPD Point. Maximum of 4 accredited points per filing period via pro bono.
Legal basis
Administrative Resolution No. (29) of 2024 — Adopting Voluntary Legal Services as an Accredited Continuing Legal Professional Development Activity, issued by the Legal Affairs Department, Government of Dubai.
For more information: probono@legal.dubai.gov.ae · +971 4 353 3337

CLPD REQUIREMENT RELIEF

Exemption / Extension request

Submit your exemption or extension application
Applications are reviewed on a case-by-case basis by the CLPD Affairs Committee. Provide full supporting documentation for your circumstances.
Exemption/Extension Application →
Who may apply for exemption?
•Serious illness preventing attendance during the filing period
•Maternity or paternity leave
•Extended travel outside UAE for professional reasons
•Other exceptional circumstances accepted by the Committee
Who may apply for extension?
•Practitioners who have made significant progress but need additional time
•Exceptional unforeseen circumstances occurring close to the deadline
•New entrants to the roll during the filing period
ℹ️
CLPD requirements may be amended per the procedures set by the Department. All amendments are made on a case-by-case basis through the CLPD Affairs Committee. There is no guarantee that an application will be approved.
For assistance: Training.Development@legal.dubai.gov.ae · +971 4 353 3337
